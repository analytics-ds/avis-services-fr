## The Business Bank Account: Obligations and Stakes

The **professional bank account** is one of the first tools to put in place when creating a business. For companies (SARL, SAS, SA, SCI), opening a separate account in the name of the legal entity is a legal requirement. For sole traders and micro-entrepreneurs, it is only mandatory beyond 10,000 euros in revenue over two consecutive years, but strongly recommended from day one.

A dedicated business account simplifies bookkeeping, smooths relations with the tax administration and protects the director's personal assets in case of dispute.

### Key selection criteria for a business bank

Before choosing a **business bank**, five criteria matter most:

- Monthly **account fees**
- Access to **financing** (overdraft, short-term credit, factoring)
- Integration with **accounting tools** (Pennylane, QuadraCompta, Dext)
- **Customer service availability** (hours, contact channels)
- **Visa Business card features** (spending limits, multi-user card management)

## Qonto: The Reference Neobank for Businesses

**Qonto** was founded in 2016 by Alexandre Prot and Steve Anavi. The French fintech is now Europe's leading professional neobank with over 600,000 clients in more than 30 countries.

### Key characteristics

- **Plans**: Solo (€9/month), Essential (€39/month), Business (€119/month), Enterprise (€249/month)
- **Transfers**: unlimited SEPA on all plans, SWIFT available as an option
- **Cards**: physical and virtual Visa Business, team expense management from the app
- **Accounting**: native integration with Pennylane, Dext, Sage, Cegid
- **Regulation**: payment institution licensed by the ACPR, funds ringfenced at BNP Paribas

Qonto stands out for the quality of its user interface and mobile app, consistently ranked best in class in specialist comparisons. Its 4.5/5 Trustpilot score (60,000+ reviews) reflects strong customer satisfaction.

## Shine and Other Neobank Options

### Shine

**Shine** was founded in 2018 and acquired by Société Générale in 2020, giving it enhanced financial solidity compared to fully independent neobanks. The company had over 130,000 clients in 2025.

Its positioning targets **freelancers, sole traders and micro-businesses**, with plans at €7.90, €14.90 and €29.90 per month. The app includes an income declaration section and a social charge simulation tool, particularly appreciated by auto-entrepreneurs.

### Propulse by Crédit Agricole

**Propulse by CA** is Crédit Agricole's digital offering for sole traders and small businesses, launched in 2021. At €8/month, it combines a professional current account, a Visa Business card and simplified access to local Crédit Agricole advisors.

## Traditional Banks: When to Choose Them

Online and neobanks remain limited on one essential point: access to **business credit**. Most businesses eventually need financing: equipment investment, working capital, commercial lease guarantees.

### BNP Paribas Pro

**BNP Paribas Pro** is the benchmark traditional bank for SMEs and micro-businesses. It offers a complete service range: current account, short and long-term credit, factoring, credit insurance and payment solutions (POS terminals, e-commerce). Monthly packages start at 15-25 euros with a dedicated advisor assigned at account opening.

### Crédit Mutuel Pro and Banque Populaire Pro

**Crédit Mutuel Pro** and **Banque Populaire Pro** stand out through their cooperative model and proximity to local entrepreneurs. Crédit Mutuel is regularly ranked France's top bank in professional client satisfaction surveys. Banque Populaire is historically the bank of SMEs with a network of over 3,200 branches.
