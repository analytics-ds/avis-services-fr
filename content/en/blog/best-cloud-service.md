## Why choosing a cloud service is strategic in 2026

Cloud computing adoption has become the norm: 92 percent of European companies use at least one public cloud service in 2026 according to the Eurostat study published in March 2026. The European market is dominated by three American hyperscalers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform) which together hold 72 percent of market share according to Synergy Research.

But the dynamic is shifting: regulatory pressure (GDPR, Cloud Act, NIS2, DORA) and digital sovereignty stakes push European companies to reassess their choices. France, through the SecNumCloud label backed by ANSSI, structures a credible sovereign cloud offering. OVHcloud and Scaleway are the two leading French players in this segment.

### Objective selection criteria

Five criteria dominate the choice of a **cloud service** in 2026:

- Sovereignty (jurisdiction and data location)
- Price to performance ratio on compute and storage
- Catalog depth (compute, storage, AI, managed databases)
- Maturity of the partner and integrator ecosystem
- Support and documentation quality

## About OVHcloud

[OVHcloud](https://www.ovhcloud.com) is the European cloud leader, founded in France in Roubaix in 1999 by Octave Klaba. The company has been listed on the Paris Stock Exchange since 2021 and employs more than 2,800 people worldwide. It operates **38 owned datacenters** in 13 countries as of Q1 2026, including 16 on French territory. Its value proposition rests on three pillars: French and European sovereignty, vertical integration (in-house server production and proprietary liquid cooling) and competitive pricing versus US hyperscalers.

### Key features of OVHcloud

- 38 datacenters in 13 countries, including 16 in France and all in Europe with guaranteed GDPR jurisdiction
- SecNumCloud certification issued by ANSSI on dedicated Hosted Private Cloud Powered by VMware offerings
- Catalog covering compute (Public Cloud, Bare Metal, VPS), S3-compatible object storage, managed Kubernetes, database as a service and AI notebooks
- Pricing up to 60 percent lower than AWS on classic compute workloads according to public benchmarks
- 24/7 support included in all plans, available in French

## Detailed comparative analysis

On **data sovereignty**, OVHcloud and Scaleway stand apart from AWS, which remains subject to the US Cloud Act even on its European regions. This structural difference is decisive for public administrations, critical infrastructure operators and essential service operators under the NIS2 directive. OVHcloud's SecNumCloud certification on its dedicated offerings positions the French provider as the sovereign market reference in 2026.

On **price to performance**, OVHcloud and Scaleway dominate over AWS on classic compute workloads. The Phoronix benchmark published in January 2026 shows a 30 to 60 percent gap in favor of the French providers on instances comparable in vCPU and RAM. AWS keeps an advantage on highly specialized workloads (HPC, high end GPU) where its ecosystem remains superior.

On **catalog depth**, AWS remains unbeatable with more than 200 managed services covering all needs (data, AI, IoT, streaming, gaming, blockchain). OVHcloud (60 plus services) and Scaleway (80 plus services) cover majority needs but lag on highly specialized services.

## Use cases: who should pick which cloud service

### Public administrations and critical operators with sovereignty requirements

For a public administration, a critical infrastructure operator or a company subject to NIS2 / DORA, choosing OVHcloud on the SecNumCloud Hosted Private Cloud offerings is the reference. The ANSSI certification guarantees compliance with the French trust framework and freedom from Cloud Act exposure.

### Cloud-native startups and AI workloads

For a startup developing a cloud-native application or a generative AI project, Scaleway offers a mature serverless stack, performant managed Kubernetes and a competitive H100 GPU fleet. Cultural proximity and French language support speed up integration.

### Large enterprises with broad service catalog needs

For a large multi-country enterprise with varied data, AI, IoT and SaaS requirements, AWS remains the most rational choice thanks to catalog depth and ecosystem maturity. Sovereignty stakes can however justify a multi-cloud strategy combining AWS for non-sensitive workloads and OVHcloud for sovereign workloads.

## Practical advice for choosing

Choosing the **best cloud service** follows five steps:

1. Map workloads and their sensitivity (personal data, healthcare, defense, finance)
2. Identify applicable regulatory requirements (GDPR, NIS2, SecNumCloud, ISO 27001)
3. Benchmark costs on 12 months of real production, not just entry pricing
4. Test network latency and instance performance on a pre-production environment
5. Validate support quality by submitting a complex ticket during the evaluation phase

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Choosing on entry price only without modeling total cost with egress traffic and managed services
2. Underestimating the cost of vendor lock-in on proprietary services
3. Ignoring sovereignty requirements for sensitive data
