## The Professional Cleaning Market in France in 2026

**Professional cleaning** is a structured and heavily regulated sector in France. With over 20 billion euros in annual revenue and around 500,000 employees working for more than 17,000 companies, cleaning is one of the country's largest private employers.

Demand is driven by several converging trends: reinforced hygiene requirements following the Covid-19 period, growing environmental standards in tender documents, and the widespread outsourcing of support functions in businesses.

### Key sector figures

- **20.3 billion euros** in revenue in 2024 (source: FEP, 2024)
- **500,000 employees** active in cleaning companies
- **17,000 companies** operating in the French market
- **80% of employees** work part-time
- The top five groups account for approximately **40%** of market share

## Onet: Historical Leader in Professional Cleaning

**Onet** was founded in 1860, making it one of the oldest service companies in France. The family-owned group (Reinier family) is today a multi-service player operating in cleaning, security, engineering and occupant services.

### Key characteristics

- **Revenue**: approximately 2 billion euros, of which 1.3 billion from cleaning
- **Workforce**: 75,000 employees in France
- **Sectors covered**: office/tertiary, industry, healthcare, transport, nuclear
- **Certifications**: NF Services Propreté, ISO 9001, ISO 14001 (environment)
- **Geographic presence**: over 350 branches across the country

Onet stands out through its ability to handle complex markets (hospitals, food production, nuclear plants) and its decentralized regional organization, which ensures local presence while drawing on national resources.

## ISS France and GSF: Two Strong Competitors

### ISS France

**ISS France** is the French subsidiary of Danish group ISS A/S, present in over 30 countries with revenues exceeding 10 billion euros globally. In France, ISS positions its offer around **integrated facility management**: cleaning, catering, reception and technical maintenance bundled in a single contract.

This model suits large companies and public organizations looking to reduce the number of service providers. ISS France employs around 45,000 people in France and holds NF Services Propreté certification.

### GSF

**GSF** is a French family-owned group, founded in 1963, employing around 60,000 people with revenue of approximately 1.4 billion euros. Its positioning is resolutely generalist, with a strong presence in office buildings, retail, mass distribution and public facilities.

GSF's family structure provides organizational stability and a proximity service culture valued by SMEs. The group has 150 branches and also covers French overseas territories.

## Elior Services and Derichebourg: Sector Specialists

### Elior Services

**Elior Services** is the cleaning division of the Elior Group, primarily known for collective catering. With 6,000 employees dedicated to cleaning, Elior Services operates mainly in healthcare facilities, care homes, university campuses and industrial food processing sites.

### Derichebourg Multiservices

**Derichebourg Multiservices** focuses on industrial, aerospace, defense and energy sectors. With 30,000 employees, the group provides industrial cleaning, decontamination and technical maintenance for high-constraint sites (Airbus, EDF, SNCF).

<details>
<summary>How much does a professional cleaning company charge for offices?</summary>

The hourly rate for standard office cleaning is between 18 and 28 euros HT in 2026, depending on frequency, floor area and location. Annual contracts allow negotiated flat-rate pricing. One-time operations such as end-of-lease or deep cleaning are billed between 30 and 50 euros HT per hour.

<details>
<summary>How to choose a professional cleaning company?</summary>

Four criteria are key: certifications obtained (ISO 9001, NF Services Propreté AFNOR), civil liability insurance coverage, sector-specific experience and client follow-up quality (reporting, dedicated contact). Verify compliance with the French collective agreement for cleaning companies (CCN Propreté).

</details>

<details>
<summary>What certifications should be required from a cleaning company?</summary>

The NF Services Propreté certification issued by AFNOR is the French benchmark. It guarantees compliance with quality, safety and environmental standards. ISO 9001 certifies a quality management system. The Ecocert or EU Ecolabel indicates the use of environmentally responsible products, an increasingly common requirement in public and private tenders.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the difference between one-time cleaning and a maintenance contract?</summary>

One-time cleaning covers a single operation: deep clean, window cleaning, post-construction cleaning. A maintenance contract schedules regular visits (daily, weekly) at a defined frequency, generally more cost-effective per hour. Most mid-sized companies opt for an annual contract for better budget predictability.

</details>
