## Document scanning: definition and issues in 2026

**Document scanning** refers to the process of converting a physical document (contract, invoice, archive, form) into a usable digital file. It covers two distinct levels: simple image scanning (scan to PDF or JPG) and scanning with character recognition (OCR), which makes the document content indexable and editable.

In 2026, scanning has become a legal and organisational necessity for many businesses. In France, the Essoc law of 2018 allows the destruction of paper originals after scanning under strict conditions, and the decree of 24 December 2022 extended the e-invoicing obligation for VAT-registered businesses.

### Why scan documents?

- Reduction in physical storage costs: one linear metre of archives represents approximately 2,000 documents and costs between €15 and €40/year in storage.
- Instant access and search: a document scanned with OCR can be found in seconds via full-text search.
- Security and backup: the risk of loss from disaster (fire, flood) is eliminated through digital duplication.
- Regulatory compliance: certain sectors (banking, insurance, healthcare) have legal obligations for the digital retention of documents.

## Adobe Scan and Microsoft Lens: the best free mobile apps

### Adobe Scan

Adobe Scan is the most complete mobile scanning app available free of charge on iOS and Android. It features automatic perspective correction, document edge detection, real-time OCR, and direct export to Adobe Acrobat. Scanned documents are stored in Adobe Document Cloud and accessible from all devices.

The app is completely free for standard individual use. Advanced features (PDF editing, electronic signature, collaboration) require an Adobe Acrobat subscription (€23.99/month).

### Microsoft Lens

Microsoft Lens stands out through its native integration with the Microsoft 365 suite. A scanned document can be exported directly as a PDF, Word document, PowerPoint presentation, or OneNote note. The app also offers a whiteboard mode for scanning presentations and diagrams. Microsoft Lens is free and available on iOS, Android, and Windows.

For Microsoft 365 users, this is the most coherent option in terms of digital ecosystem.

### Google Drive scan

The scan function of the Google Drive app allows a document to be scanned directly as a PDF with built-in OCR. The file is automatically saved to Google Drive, facilitating sharing and collaboration. This solution is particularly suited to Google Workspace users.

## CamScanner Pro and advanced mobile apps

CamScanner is one of the most downloaded mobile scanning apps in the world, with over 500 million installations. The free version is limited (watermarks on exports, restricted storage). The Pro version, available at €4.99/month or €35/year, removes limitations and adds batch processing features, advanced OCR, and electronic signature.

CamScanner is particularly suited to mobile professionals (sales representatives, consultants) who regularly scan large numbers of documents from their smartphone.

## ABBYY FineReader and Adobe Acrobat: professional desktop solutions

### ABBYY FineReader

ABBYY FineReader is the market reference for precision OCR on desktop. The software recognises over 200 languages and scripts, processes complex documents (tables, forms, multi-column documents) with accuracy exceeding 99% on good-quality documents. ABBYY FineReader Standard is available from €69/year on subscription.

The software is particularly suited to legal, notarial, and accounting professions that process large volumes of contractual or administrative documents.

### Adobe Acrobat

Adobe Acrobat Pro DC (€23.99/month) is Adobe's complete document suite, including scanning, OCR, PDF editing, electronic signature, and secure sharing. Integration with Adobe Scan and Adobe Document Cloud makes it a coherent solution for organisations that want to unify their document workflow.

## Zeendoc, Iron Mountain and enterprise DMS solutions

### Zeendoc

Zeendoc is a French Document Management System (DMS) specifically designed for SMEs. The platform offers multichannel capture (scanner, file import, email), automatic OCR, intelligent classification by document type, and a full-text search engine. Data is hosted in France, guaranteeing GDPR compliance.

Zeendoc pricing starts at around €150/month for an SME with fewer than 10 users. An electronic signature module and a collaborative portal are available as options.

### Iron Mountain

Iron Mountain is the global leader in bulk document archiving and scanning. The service is primarily aimed at large companies and public sector organisations that want to digitise large volumes of historical archives. Processing is carried out in secure scanning centres, with options for certified destruction of originals after scanning. Prices are established on quote based on volume and processing specifications.

### Numen

Numen (formerly SGA, a Crédit Mutuel subsidiary) is a French specialist in archive digitisation and document management for the banking, insurance, and medical sectors. The company also offers certified digital safe deposit box services and legally admissible archiving.

## How to choose a scanning service by profile

The choice of a scanning service depends primarily on document volume, the level of OCR accuracy required, and security constraints.

| Profile | Recommended solution | Monthly budget |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| Occasional individual | Adobe Scan or Google Drive scan | Free |
| Mobile professional | CamScanner Pro | €4.99/month |
| Legal or accounting firm | ABBYY FineReader | ~€6/month |
| SME up to 50 employees | Zeendoc | €150 to €400/month |
| Large company or public sector | Iron Mountain or Numen | On quote |

For sensitive documents (contracts, payslips, medical data), only GDPR-certified solutions with European hosting guarantee full compliance. Adobe Scan and Microsoft Lens, while excellent for daily use, store data in US cloud infrastructure subject to the Cloud Act.
