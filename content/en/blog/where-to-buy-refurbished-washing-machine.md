## The refurbished washing machine market in France

The **refurbished washing machine** market has become one of the fastest-growing segments in the French second-hand appliance sector. Rising prices for new appliances and growing environmental awareness are driving more consumers toward professionally refurbished units rather than new or uncontrolled second-hand purchases.

A mid-range new washing machine (7 to 8 kg, energy class B) costs between 350 and 700 euros in 2026. A refurbished equivalent from a professional retailer can be obtained for 200 to 500 euros, with the same 12-month legal warranty. This price gap of 30 to 40% is the primary driver of growth in the refurbished segment for large appliances.

### Why washing machines are well suited to refurbishment

Washing machines have several characteristics that make them particularly suitable for professional refurbishment.

- **Long service life**: a quality washing machine is designed to last 10 to 15 years. A five or six-year-old refurbished unit still has significant remaining lifespan.
- **Available spare parts**: major brands (Bosch, Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, Miele) maintain spare parts availability for at least 10 years after a model is discontinued.
- **Repairable faults**: the most common washing machine failures (door seal, drain pump, motor brushes) are identifiable and repairable by trained technicians without replacing the whole appliance.
- **Reduced environmental impact**: manufacturing a new washing machine generates between 150 and 250 kg of CO₂ equivalent over its lifecycle. Refurbishment avoids this carbon footprint by extending the life of an existing appliance.

As explored in our comparison of [refurbished vs used vs new appliances](/en/blog/refurbished-vs-used-vs-new/), professional refurbishment combines the pricing advantage of second-hand goods with the reliability of new, thanks to documented quality control processes.

## Murfy: the large home appliance refurbishment specialist

Founded in 2018 in France, Murfy is a company specialising in the **repair and refurbishment of large home appliances**: washing machines, dishwashers, refrigerators, ovens and tumble dryers. Its model differs fundamentally from generalist platforms through expertise focused exclusively on large appliances, combined with a national network of qualified technicians providing in-home service.

Every washing machine refurbished by Murfy goes through a structured multi-step process: complete electronic and mechanical diagnosis, replacement of worn or defective parts with original or certified equivalent components, thorough internal and external cleaning, and a series of functional tests before resale. The appliance is systematically sold with a 12-month parts and labour warranty.

### Key strengths of Murfy for buying a washing machine

- **Large appliance expertise**: Murfy only sells and repairs large home appliances. This specialisation translates into deep knowledge of common failures by brand and model, and a targeted spare parts inventory for this segment.
- **Delivery and in-home installation**: the washing machine is delivered and installed by a Murfy technician, including connection to water supply and drainage. Collection of the old appliance is generally included.
- **In-home after-sales service under warranty**: in the event of a breakdown within the 12-month warranty period, a Murfy technician visits the buyer's home at no additional cost.
- **Varied brand catalogue**: Bosch, Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, Siemens and Beko are among the available models, with prices between 200 and 500 euros for the vast majority of references.

Murfy also offers a [home appliance repair service](https://murfy.fr/) independent of purchasing a refurbished unit, reflecting a business model built around extending the life of appliances rather than systematic replacement.

## Back Market, Darty and FNAC: the generalist alternatives

### Back Market

Back Market is a marketplace founded in 2014 that brings together offers from professional refurbishment sellers on a single platform. Originally positioned in consumer electronics (smartphones, computers, tablets), the platform has expanded its catalogue to large home appliances including washing machines. Prices range from 150 to 550 euros with a systematic 12-month warranty.

Back Market's advantage lies in the transparency of its seller rating system (quality score displayed on each product page) and the breadth of available models. The main limitation for a washing machine purchase: delivery and installation are not systematically included in the listed price, and after-sales support goes through the third-party seller listed on the platform rather than through a physical store network.

### Darty Reconditionné and FNAC Reconditionné

Both Darty and FNAC operate dedicated refurbished sections integrated into their respective websites. Appliances are refurbished by their in-house teams or certified partners. Prices are generally somewhat higher than Murfy or Back Market (200 to 800 euros for a washing machine), but their physical store network is a genuine advantage for buyers who prefer to handle after-sales matters in person rather than remotely.

Both retailers are well suited to buyers who are less comfortable with a fully online purchase for a large appliance, and for whom access to a physical store represents a meaningful confidence factor.

## Which platform for which buyer profile?

### First-time buyer or mobile tenant

For someone furnishing a first flat on an optimised budget without technical expertise, Murfy offers the best available balance: controlled pricing, delivery with installation by a technician, and in-home after-sales service that neutralises the risks associated with buying a second-hand appliance. Back Market is an alternative if the budget is even tighter, provided delivery and installation costs are included or covered.

### Environmentally conscious buyer

Manufacturing a 7 kg washing machine involves extracting and processing materials whose end-of-life recycling is complex (steel, technical plastics, electronic wiring). Choosing a refurbished appliance extends the life of an existing unit and avoids the carbon footprint of manufacturing a new one. Murfy, as a French company whose core business is repair, directly embodies this circular economy approach.

### Experienced buyer on a tight budget

Leboncoin remains an accessible option for buyers who can assess the condition of an appliance during an in-person purchase. Prices can drop below 100 euros for a functional washing machine. This option is best reserved for buyers with sufficient technical knowledge to diagnose common defects (abnormal noise, excessive vibration, leaks) and, if necessary, to carry out or commission a repair.

## How to choose a refurbished washing machine: essential criteria

Regardless of the platform chosen, four criteria should be checked systematically before any purchase.

| Criterion | What to check |
|-----------|---------------|
| Refurbishment grade | Grade A (as-new condition), Grade B (minor visible signs of use), Grade C (visible marks, lower price) |
| Warranty | 12 months minimum from a professional seller, covering parts and labour |
| Brand and parts availability | Prefer Bosch, Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, Siemens (parts available for 10+ years) |
| Capacity suited to the household | 7 to 8 kg for 2 to 3 people, 9 to 10 kg for families of 4 or more |
| Energy class | Target class B minimum (former A++ or A+++ label) to limit electricity and water consumption |

### Mistakes to avoid when buying a refurbished washing machine

1. **Buying without a written warranty**: a washing machine undergoes intensive mechanical cycles. The cost of a repair (door seal, drum bearing, drain pump) can exceed the purchase price if no warranty covers parts and labour.
2. **Overlooking delivery and installation costs**: a washing machine weighs between 60 and 100 kg. Delivery with porterage and installation can cost 50 to 100 euros when not included in the listed price.
3. **Under-sizing the capacity**: choosing a 5 kg machine for a household of 4 means running more cycles, which increases water and energy consumption and accelerates wear on the appliance.
