## The senior home care market in 2026

1.5 million elderly people in France receive home care, according to DREES 2024 figures. The number rises by about 4 per cent every year, pushed by two converging trends: a population that keeps aging (the over-75s will reach 13 million by 2050) and a growing rejection of nursing homes, considered too impersonal or too expensive by 72 per cent of families in a 2024 Silver Alliance survey.

Behind the term **senior home care services** sits a very wide spectrum. Helping with weekly shopping has little in common with night watch for a person with cognitive disorders. Services, required qualifications and rates vary accordingly. Understanding these differences prevents unpleasant surprises on both the bill and the service quality side.

### A regulated and structured sector

Two accreditation types frame the sector:

- **Quality accreditation** (mandatory) for services to vulnerable people.
- **Departmental authorization** for medico-social services.

The National Quality Charter also demands a minimum staff training, service continuity and a confidentiality clause. A provider that fails to meet these benchmarks simply operates outside the legal framework.

## Services offered

**Home care services** cover a wide scope, from light material help to support with essential daily activities. The typical basket for a GIR 4 senior mobilizes 2.5 different services on average (DREES 2023 figures).

### Main services

- **Housekeeping**: cleaning, ironing, laundry.
- **Meal assistance**: preparation, eating support, shopping.
- **Bathing and dressing**: reserved for qualified care assistants.
- **Medical and administrative support**: appointments, paperwork, prescription follow-up.
- **Night presence**: help with night rising, monitoring.
- **Tele-alarm and remote assistance**: emergency button, fall detection.
- **Meal delivery**: daily delivery of balanced meals.
- **Minor DIY and gardening**: comprehensive personal services.

Watch out: not every service is accessible to every worker. Bathing, transfer assistance or medication administration strictly require a state-certified care assistant. Assigning these tasks to a regular home helper is not only risky, it is also illegal.

### Employment modes

Three modes exist, with very different administrative implications:

| Mode | Who employs? | Advantages | Drawbacks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Service provider | The accredited service | Simplicity, no HR | Higher rate (26 to 32 euros/h) |
| Mandate | Senior (via service) | More autonomy, mid rate | Partial employer responsibility |
| Direct | Senior directly | Lower rate (14 to 20 euros/h) | Full responsibility, payroll management |

The provider mode dominates by far (68 per cent of hours per DREES data). Direct employment stays relevant for families able to handle payroll and admin, or those with a long-term relationship with a specific worker.

## 2026 rates

**2026 rates** for home care rose 4 to 6 per cent compared to 2024, a direct consequence of the sector wage revaluation (collective bargaining agreement 43 and following). The grid remains fragmented by mode, region and service type.

### Indicative price grid

See the price table at the start of the article. Provider-mode rates include social charges. In direct mode, count about 40 per cent employer charges on top of the gross hourly wage.

### Weekend and holiday surcharge

Sunday and bank holiday interventions carry a 25 to 50 per cent surcharge depending on the applicable collective agreement. A point often underestimated in initial budgets, which can inflate the monthly bill by 10 to 15 per cent for intensive care plans.

## Financial aid

Several schemes significantly cut the real cost for seniors and their families. Combining them wisely can bring the out-of-pocket rate from a full 32 euros per hour down to 3 or 4 euros in the most-aided cases.

### Personalized Autonomy Allowance (APA)

Home APA is the main aid for seniors losing autonomy. It is granted to people aged 60 and over classified in GIR 1 to 4 (AGGIR scale). Maximum monthly amounts in 2026 reach:

- GIR 1: 1,986 euros per month.
- GIR 2: 1,621 euros per month.
- GIR 3: 1,172 euros per month.
- GIR 4: 782 euros per month.

Depending on income, the person pays a co-payment from 0 to 90 per cent.

### Tax credit

Any individual employer or personal service client gets a **50 per cent tax credit** on expenses, capped at 12,000 euros per year (with family-based increases). The credit is combinable with APA, which makes the real difference.

> "The personal services tax credit represents a fiscal expense of 6.5 billion euros per year. It halves the real cost for eligible households."
> French Court of Auditors, 2023 report

### Other aid

- **Social home assistance**: for low-income seniors not eligible for APA.
- **Pension funds**: CNAV and special schemes provide up to 3,000 euros per year.
- **Mutual insurance**: some partially cover home care.
- **ANAH top-ups**: aid for home adaptation.

On the last point, adapting the home often goes hand in hand with the arrival of an outside worker. Replacing aging appliances with more ergonomic equipment regularly ends up on the action list. Buying [refurbished appliances to equip a kitchen](/en/blog/equip-kitchen-refurbished/) or installing an eye-level oven can cost half as much as new, and free up budget for more intervention hours.

## How to choose a home care service

The choice of a provider combines three demands: legal security, service quality and a price fitting the care plan. The classic mistake is to decide on the hourly rate alone. A provider 3 euros cheaper with 80 per cent yearly staff turnover costs much more in the end than a stable service.

### Essential criteria

- Check **quality accreditation** or departmental authorization.
- Request **at least 3 comparative quotes** in detail.
- Confirm **training** and staff turnover.
- Check **service continuity** in case of regular staff absence.
- Carefully read the **contract**: termination clauses, billing.
- Consult **quality labels** (Cap Handeo, NF Afnor Service).

### Steps to set up home care

1. Needs assessment by a professional (APA service or provider).
2. Selection of 2 to 3 providers and quote requests.
3. Meeting with potential staff to check human fit.
4. Contract signing after full clause analysis.
5. Gradual rollout, with adjustments from the first week.
6. Review at 1 month to adapt if needed.

## Taking care of caregivers

The arrival of outside help brings relief, but the family caregiver stays engaged: appointments, admin, coordination with workers. 58 per cent of caregivers report high fatigue levels per DREES, and 30 per cent suffer from at least one pathology worsened by the role. Taking time to recover is not a luxury, it is a condition for lasting. A [Californian massage](/en/blog/californian-massage/) session or an hour spent in a [hammam](/en/blog/hammam-benefits/) measurably lowers cortisol, and these are often the first things caregivers drop for lack of time.

<summary>How do you apply for home APA?</summary>

The APA application is filed with the departmental council via a single file including identity and income documents and a medical certificate. A medico-social team then evaluates the degree of autonomy loss (GIR). Average processing time is 2 to 3 months in 2026.
