Buying a refurbished phone is one of the most cost-effective ways to upgrade a smartphone without paying full price. In 2026, the refurbished market accounts for over 20% of smartphone sales in France, driven by increasingly high-quality offerings and stronger legal warranties. The key is knowing where to buy, which criteria to trust and which pitfalls to avoid.

## Refurbished Phone: What the Term Really Means

A refurbished phone is a used device restored to working condition by a professional. Unlike second-hand devices sold as-is, refurbishment involves a complete technical diagnosis, replacement of faulty parts (screen, battery, connectors), thorough cleaning and a factory reset.

Each device is then graded according to its cosmetic condition:

| Grade | Cosmetic condition | Recommended use |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Excellent (A+ / Like new) | No visible marks | Gift, intensive use |
| Very good condition (A / B) | Micro-scratches invisible to the naked eye | Standard daily use |
| Good condition (C) | Slight visible scratches | Tight budget, secondary use |

The grade does not reflect functional quality: a grade C device works exactly as well as a grade A. Only the exterior appearance differs. For buyers less concerned with aesthetics, grade C offers the best value for money.

Before committing, [the question of refurbished vs used vs new](/en/blog/refurbished-vs-used-vs-new/) is worth exploring: the differences in warranties and risks are significant depending on the purchase channel.

## The Best Online Platforms for Buying Refurbished

The market is dominated by a few players whose reliability has been established over several years. Here are the three essential references.

### Back Market: The Market's Generalist Leader

Back Market aggregates stock from hundreds of certified refurbishers. Each seller is rated by buyers and regularly audited by the platform.

- **Catalogue**: over 1,000 references (iPhone, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, Xiaomi, Huawei)
- **Warranty**: 12 months, renewable
- **Return**: 30 days, no questions asked
- **Strengths**: wide choice, competitive pricing, seller rating transparency, mobile app
- **Weakness**: prices vary between refurbishers for the same model — compare before buying

Back Market suits buyers who want the widest selection and price flexibility.

### Recommerce: The French Specialist

Recommerce is the leader in 100% French refurbishment, with its own workshops in France. It displays the carbon footprint saved for each device and offers the longest warranty on the mainstream market.

- **Catalogue**: focused on iPhone, Samsung Galaxy and Google Pixel
- **Warranty**: 3 years (36 months), covers functional defects
- **Return**: 30 days
- **Strengths**: environmental transparency, French reconditioning, premium warranty
- **Weakness**: more limited range, prices slightly above market average

Recommerce suits buyers who prioritise warranty duration and the French supply chain.

### CertiDeal: The Longest Warranty

CertiDeal, a specialist French operator, offers a 30-month warranty on all devices and a 30-day trial period.

- **Catalogue**: smartphones, tablets, MacBooks
- **Warranty**: 30 months
- **Return**: 30 days
- **Strengths**: exceptional warranty duration, established reliability, consistent refurbishment quality
- **Weakness**: smaller catalogue than Back Market, slightly above-average pricing

## Physical Retailers and Operators: A Solid Option

Buying a refurbished phone in a physical store allows you to see and test the device before paying — an advantage that e-commerce cannot replicate.

Main options:

- **Fnac Refurbished / Darty Refurbished**: in-store and online selection, 2-year warranty, in-store returns possible within 30 days. Limited stock but reliable sourcing.
- **SFR / Orange**: refurbished devices with or without a plan, 2-year warranty, stock focused on premium iPhone and Samsung models.
- **Easy Cash**: network of physical shops with on-site refurbishment. Competitive pricing on entry-level and mid-range devices, strong regional presence.

The main disadvantage remains the more restricted choice compared to online platforms. For other appliance categories — such as when looking to [buy a refurbished washing machine](/en/blog/where-to-buy-refurbished-washing-machine/) — physical retailers often complement specialist online platforms.

## iPhone Refurbished or Android Refurbished: How to Choose

The choice between iOS and Android does not change the refurbished buying logic, but a few points are worth noting.

**Refurbished iPhone**

Apple guarantees iOS updates for 5 to 6 years, including on older models. A refurbished iPhone 12 still receives iOS 18 in 2026. Resale value remains high. Key caveat: models prior to iPhone 12 do not support 5G.

**Refurbished Android**

Entry prices are lower: Samsung Galaxy A or Xiaomi Redmi models start below €100 refurbished. Samsung guarantees 4 years of OS updates on its Galaxy S line, making it the safest Android choice when buying refurbished.

For a budget below €200, Android clearly offers more choice. Between €300 and €500, refurbished iPhone and Samsung Galaxy S converge in terms of quality-to-durability ratio.

## Warranty, Return Rights and Recourse in Case of Breakdown

Since 1 January 2022, French law requires a minimum legal warranty of 6 months for refurbished products sold by professionals. This warranty covers conformity defects.

The main platforms go well beyond this legal minimum:

| Platform | Commercial warranty | Return period |
|----------|--------------------|--------------:|
| Back Market | 12 months | 30 days |
| Smaaart | 24 months | 30 days |
| Fnac Refurbished | 24 months | 30 days |
| CertiDeal | 30 months | 30 days |
| Recommerce | 36 months | 30 days |

In the event of a breakdown under warranty, the process is identical to that of a new product: online or phone declaration, device return, repair or replacement. Keeping the purchase receipt is essential to exercise your rights.

## Five Criteria for Choosing a Seller with Confidence

Before confirming a purchase, five points should be systematically checked:

1. **Warranty duration**: aim for at least 12 months, ideally 24 months or more
2. **Return period**: 30 days is the market standard — anything less is a warning sign
3. **Clearly defined grade**: the description should be precise, with representative photos
4. **Seller ratings**: on Back Market, check the individual seller's rating, not just the platform's overall score
5. **Battery condition**: ensure residual capacity is disclosed (80% minimum recommended) or that the battery has been replaced
